The Startup Science

All the topics covered during the programme.

We will share the content and slides for the topics after each sprint.

Sprint One

Code Institute Startup Academy Introduction

We introduce the academy, clarify the expectations, and explain all of its parts and pieces.

Do you have what it takes to be a founder?

We explain what it takes to be an entrepreneur and the founder of a successful startup. We talk about the reality of being an entrepreneur.

Sprint Two

Is your startup idea worth it?

We walk you through how to assess ideas and how you should think about your startups.

How do you talk to users and/or potential users?

We provide a structure for asking questions and collecting feedback from users and potential users.

Sprint Three

Naming your startup and the initial steps to set up your brand.

We talk about the importance of choosing a great name and the initial designs of the startup brand.

Tools and services to get you started and for which ones you should pay.

There are a lot of services out there, and it can be overwhelming to choose which ones to use when starting up. We share the best and most affordable tools to get started.

Sprint Four

What is an MVP (Minimum Viable Product), and how do you build one?

We explain what an MVP is and what is not. We also share the best way to build an MVP and get the first users as a pre-launch startup.

Do you need a co-founder and how would you find one?

We talk about how to find a co-founder, where co-founders from top companies meet, what investors look for in cofounding teams, how to split equity with co-founders, and if a co-founder is needed.

Sprint Five

How to work with co-founders.

We talk about the importance of creating a successful working relationship with co-founders and establishing processes to optimize the strengths of your team.

How to set the price for your product/service?

We go over the basics of pricing and monetization, how it will affect customer acquisition, and how to improve it through a few standard best practices.

Sprint Six

Pitching your startup and how to do it right.

We go over how to summarise the idea and communicate it to an investor. We'll share tips on how to craft a precise and compact pitch that grabs the audience's attention.

You’ve raised your first investment round, now what?

We clarify what it means to raise funding and what are mandatory steps to follow once that happens.
